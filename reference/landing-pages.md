# Reference — Landing Pages

Use when building a marketing / landing page, hero, or marketing section (SaaS, consumer, agency).

## Signature moves
- **Warm off-white canvas + ONE rationed accent.** Page sits on warm paper-grey (`#F2F1EE`–`#F5F4F1`); a single committed brand color does all the work (grass `#2FBF6B`, electric blue `#2E7DF6`, warm orange `#F26B2A`, violet `#7C5CFC`). Never a rainbow on the page chrome.
- **The textured surface IS the centerpiece** — either edge-to-edge nature photography / painterly art, or a grainy saturated mesh gradient with a focal glow. The layout around it stays calm.
- **The inset "app-in-a-window" frame** — float the whole hero/product inside a rounded white card on the grey canvas, or a tilted browser/app mock half-bleeding off the bottom edge.
- **Mixed-script display headline** — big near-black headline where ONE phrase switches to accent color, an *italic serif*, or a highlighted pill-tag word with a tiny inline glyph.
- **Squircle + pill everything** — ~24px continuous-curve cards, pill nav, pill buttons. No sharp 4px corners.

## Layout & composition
- **Two hero archetypes only** (both deliberate):
  1. **Centered** — eyebrow pill → huge headline → one-line subhead → one dark CTA (or CTA + email field) → product imagery floating below, half-cut-off.
  2. **Asymmetric split** — copy stacked bottom-left on dotted-grid empty space; a tall textured gradient/photo panel fills the right ~50%.
- **Calm section rhythm** — one idea per band, big vertical breathing room. Feature blocks = single rounded cards (visual + 2-line title + subhead), not dense equal-tile grids.
- **Whitespace is intentional counterweight**, never a symptom of "ran out of content." A blank quadrant balances a heavy gradient panel.
- **Nav = floating pill**, top-center or top-left, detached from the page edge (margin all around): logo + 3–5 links + one dark/accent CTA. NEVER a full-bleed bar overlapping the hero.
- **Trust-logo row** sits quiet and monochrome at the bottom of the hero — never inside the hero copy row.

## Color & palette
- Canvas (light): warm off-white `#F2F1EE` / `#F4F3F0` (not pure `#FFF`).
- Ink: charcoal `#1A1A1A`–`#222` for headlines + primary buttons; body grey `#6B6B6B`.
- Accent: exactly one per page, saturated.
- Dark: charcoal `#161616`–`#1E1E1E` (not black), cards `#242424`, hairlines 8–12% white. Dark heroes get ONE radial glow (amber dawn from bottom / blue aurora from top), single light source, deep falloff. Dark is a first-class twin, not an afterthought.

## Surfaces — gradients / shaders / imagery (make-or-break)
Three approved treatments; none is the pale-flat-blur:
1. **Grainy mesh gradient with real depth** — saturated pastel mesh (violet→pink→sky, coral→cream) with **visible film grain** + a **clear focal bloom** the colors radiate from. Directional, with a center of gravity.
2. **Real / painterly nature imagery, edge-to-edge** — golden-hour / atmospheric (waterfalls, alpine valleys, clouds from a plane window, wildflower hills) with a dark-to-transparent scrim at the bottom for legible white text. Never stocky.
3. **Abstract directional shader panel** — vertical light-streak gradients, halftone-dot pixel mesh — high chroma, real motion-feel, as the tall split panel.

**The test:** if a gradient could be mistaken for a Tailwind `from-purple-100 to-blue-100`, it's wrong. Every reference gradient has: (a) committed saturation, (b) a directional/radial light source, (c) grain OR a crisp photographic/painterly subject.

## Typography
- Geometric/grotesk sans for structure; headlines large, heavy (semibold/bold), near-black, ~2 lines, tight leading.
- Restrained copy — body is small, grey, one short sentence.
- Signature accents: italic-serif swap mid-headline; highlighter pill-words (soft colored chip + tiny inline glyph); accent-color word swap inside a black headline.
- Stats: big tabular metric + tiny grey label beneath.

## Shape, radius & depth
- Continuous-curvature squircles (~24px cards, ~16px inner), pills on nav/buttons/badges.
- Soft, low, diffuse shadows (`0 20px 40px rgba(0,0,0,0.06)`) — floating-card lift, no hard edges.
- Hairline borders ~6–10%. Glass/blur only on overlay panels.

## Components
Pill nav · dark primary pill button (often `→`/`↗`) + ghost secondary · eyebrow/tag pill above headline · feature cards (mini chart/visual + bold title + one grey line) · `+18%` green stat deltas · floating tilted product mock half-off the bottom · monochrome trust-logo strip · small rounded app-icon tiles inside cards.

## Premium vs slop
**Premium:** one rationed accent · warm off-white canvas · one committed textured focal surface (photo or grainy directional glowing gradient) · calm composition with counterweighted negative space · squircle/pill consistency · soft diffuse shadows · short copy · dark mode as an equal twin.

**Slop to avoid:** pale evenly-lit gradient blobs (no grain/direction/glow) → reads as default CSS · text-on-flat-tint hero with no imagery · nav colliding with hero text · elements plopped dead-center with four empty corners · over-stuffed maximalism / multiple bright colors · pure `#FFF` + pure black + sharp corners + hard shadows (the bootstrap tell).

**One-line rule:** *warm canvas + ONE accent + ONE committed textured hero (real photo or grainy directional glowing mesh) + calm asymmetric-or-clean composition + squircle/pill chrome — if the hero is text on a pale flat gradient, it's slop.*
